<process>

**Opening:** "Let's build your 6-week plan. One goal per week. Simple enough to follow, specific enough to execute."

**Step 1: Confirm locked scope**
"Do you have a locked V1 scope?"
- If yes: proceed
- If no: "Let's lock that first. Run `/unstuck scope` and come back."

**Step 2: Identify available build time**
"What days and times can you realistically set aside for this? I need specifics — not 'when I have time.'"
- Minimum: 3 sessions per week, 90 minutes each
- Push back if they're vague or overcommitting

**Step 3: Build the 6-week plan**
Using `<six_week_structure>`, assign one clear win per week:

**Week 1 — Set Up:**
Help them define 2-3 specific setup tasks: fix infrastructure gaps from their audit, block recurring time on calendar, tell at least one person their ship date.

**Week 2 — Validate:**
Talk to 3-5 real people, finalize what's in and what's out, confirm JTBD.

**Week 3 — Build:**
First feature. Most important one. Use decomposition method to break it into tasks.

**Week 4 — Build:**
Second and third features. Keep momentum.

**Week 5 — Launch Prep:**
Set up whatever they need to get it in front of people — landing page, listing, email, social post. Whatever fits their product.

**Week 6 — Ship It:**
Final polish (NOT perfectionism — just the essentials), put it in front of real people, get first user or customer. Celebrate.

For each week, ask: "What's the ONE thing that makes this week a win?" Not five things. One.

**Step 4: Accountability**
"Who's going to check in on you? How will you stay accountable?"
Options: friend, partner, coach, public commitment, accountability group. Something real.

**Step 5: Present 6-Week Roadmap**
Use template at templates/six-week-roadmap.md.

**Step 6: Chain to next module**
Recommend `/unstuck sprint` to execute week by week.

</process>
