# Generate FAQ from Objections

## What This Skill Does

Takes the real objections your customers raise — the "it's too expensive," the "I don't have time," the "I've tried this before" — and transforms them into an FAQ section that does three things: reframes the objection (not just answers it), sells in your brand voice, and guides people to the right offer if you have multiple tiers.

This is not a generic FAQ generator. It uses a 4-layer objection handling framework: surface statement → underlying fear → reframe → scripted answer. The output reads like you wrote it, addresses what your customers actually say, and serves as a conversion tool — not just an information section.

## Phase 1: Objection Discovery

Ask these questions one at a time. Wait for the user's answer before moving to the next.

**If the user is stuck on any question:** Help them surface objections they haven't articulated. Most business owners hear the same 5-10 objections but haven't written them down. Prompt with common categories: time, money, skepticism, past failures, readiness, fit.

### The 6 Questions

**Q1: "What are you selling, and at what price point(s)?"**
The offer, the price, and if there's a product ladder (multiple tiers at different prices). If you have multiple tiers, list them — the FAQ needs to help people self-select the right one.

*If stuck, ask:* "What's the main thing someone can buy from you, and how much does it cost? Do you have cheaper or more expensive options too?"

**Q2: "Who's your ideal buyer?"**
Not demographics — their situation. What are they struggling with? What have they already tried that didn't work? What's the conversation in their head when they're deciding whether to buy?

*If stuck, ask:* "Think about your best customer. What were they dealing with right before they decided to work with you? What had they already tried?"

**Q3: "What objections do you hear most?"**
The actual words people use. Not sanitized versions — the real things. "It's too expensive." "I don't have time." "I've been burned by coaching before." "Can't I just figure this out on my own?" "My spouse thinks this is a waste of money." List as many as you can.

*If stuck, ask:* "What do people say right before they DON'T buy? Or what questions do they ask on sales calls that you know are really objections in disguise?"

**Q4: "What's your brand voice?"**
How do you talk? Direct and no-BS? Warm and empathetic? Technical and precise? Funny and irreverent? Give me 3-4 adjectives, or a comparison ("I sound like Brene Brown meets a PM standup, not Tony Robbins").

*If stuck, ask:* "If a friend described how you talk to clients, what would they say? What do you sound like, and what do you definitely NOT sound like?"

**Q5: "What proof do you have that this works?"**
Testimonials, case studies, your own results, credentials, data, before/after stories. This is what powers the reframes — every objection answer should land on proof, not just reassurance.

*If stuck, ask:* "What's the most compelling result you or a client has gotten? Even if it's just one person — what happened?"

**Q6: "Are there logistics questions people need answered?"**
Cancellation policy, payment plans, refund policy, how sessions are scheduled, what the time commitment is, how delivery works, whether they can pause, whether there's a trial. The boring-but-necessary stuff.

*If stuck, ask:* "After someone decides they want to buy — what practical questions come up? Scheduling, payment, cancellation, format?"

## Phase 2: Generate the FAQ Document

After gathering all 6 answers, generate a structured FAQ document with these 4 sections.

### Section 1: Objection FAQs

The main event. For each objection from Q3, produce:

**The Question**
Written in the customer's actual voice. Not "What is the return on investment?" — more like "Is this actually worth $4,500?"

**4-Layer Breakdown:**

| Layer | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Surface Objection** | What they're saying out loud |
| **Underlying Fear** | What they're actually afraid of (the real objection behind the objection) |
| **The Reframe** | Flip the frame — don't just answer, change how they see the problem |
| **Proof Point** | The specific evidence that makes the reframe credible |

**The Answer (3-5 sentences, in brand voice)**
This is the FAQ answer that goes on the page. It should:
- Acknowledge the concern (don't dismiss it)
- Reframe it (the flip)
- Land on proof or redirect to the right offer
- Sound like the user wrote it, not an AI

**Example structure:**
> "Can't I figure this out on my own?"
>
> You could. You're smart enough — that's not the question. The question is: if you were going to, why haven't you already? The gap isn't intelligence — it's infrastructure. You've been trying to build with motivation, and motivation has a shelf life of about 72 hours. [PROOF: "In the first week, my build partner helped me cut my scope from 47 features to 6. I shipped in 5 weeks." — Eric K.]

### Section 2: Cross-Sell FAQs

Questions that help people self-select the right tier. Only include if the user has multiple offers.

For each cross-sell FAQ:
- **"Which option is right for me?"** — Decision framework based on where the buyer is (I know what to build → Tier A; I'm stuck on what/how → Tier B)
- **"What's the difference between X and Y?"** — Clear differentiation by job-to-be-done, not just feature comparison
- **"Can I upgrade/downgrade later?"** — Remove the commitment fear
- **"I want X but can only afford Y"** — Guide them to the right tier without making them feel cheap

Each answer should guide, not pressure. Point people to the RIGHT tier, not the most expensive one.

### Section 3: Logistics FAQs

Straightforward answers to operational questions. No selling — just clarity and transparency.

Common logistics FAQs (include all that apply):
- How does payment work? (one-time, subscription, payment plans)
- What's the cancellation/refund policy?
- How are sessions scheduled? / What's the time commitment?
- How is the product/service delivered?
- How long do I have access?
- Can I pause my membership/subscription?
- Do you offer a trial or discovery call?
- What happens after I buy?

**Rules for logistics FAQs:**
- Answer completely in 2-3 sentences
- Include specific details (not "flexible scheduling" but "you'll get a Calendly link with 15 time slots per week")
- If there's a refund policy, state it clearly — vague refund language creates more objections than it solves

### Section 4: Objection Handling Cheat Sheet

Quick-reference table for sales calls or DMs:

| Objection | Underlying Fear | One-Line Reframe | Proof Point |
|-----------|----------------|-----------------|-------------|
| "Too expensive" | Wasting money on something that won't work | "[reframe]" | [specific result] |
| "No time" | Adding another thing that fails | "[reframe]" | [specific result] |
| ... | ... | ... | ... |

This table is for the user's reference during live conversations — not for the website.

## Rules

- **Questions must be in the customer's real voice.** "Is this worth it?" not "What is the value proposition?" If the FAQ sounds like a corporate document, it's wrong.
- **Every reframe must change the frame, not just reassure.** "I don't have time" → the reframe is NOT "we work around your schedule." It's "that feeling of having no time IS the stuck — it's a symptom, not a constraint."
- **Answers must use the user's brand voice.** If they're direct and anti-fluff, the FAQ should be too. If they're warm and empathetic, match that. Don't default to generic professional tone.
- **Never fabricate testimonials or results.** If no proof exists for a specific reframe, use [NEEDS PROOF] as a placeholder and flag it.
- **Cross-sell language guides, never pressures.** The goal is to point people to the tier that actually fits them. Pushing everyone to the most expensive option destroys trust.
- **Logistics FAQs must be complete and specific.** Vague answers ("flexible refund policy") create more anxiety than no answer at all.
- **If brand-voice-router is active,** apply the detected brand voice to all FAQ answers.
- **If humanize-ai-writing is available,** run all output through it before delivering. FAQ answers that sound like AI are worse than no FAQ at all.
- **One FAQ per concern.** Don't combine "is this worth it?" and "what if it doesn't work?" into one answer. Each objection gets its own entry.
- **Order matters.** Lead with the most common objection, not the easiest to answer.

## What This Skill Does NOT Do

- Write sales page copy (the FAQ is one section — full sales pages are a different skill)
- Create DM scripts for handling objections live (the cheat sheet helps, but scripted conversations are a separate deliverable)
- Build the FAQ into a website (it produces the content, not the HTML/implementation)
- Replace customer research (if the user doesn't know what objections they hear, they need to talk to more customers first)
- Write email sequences that handle objections over time (that's email marketing, not FAQ)
