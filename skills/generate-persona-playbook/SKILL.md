# Generate Persona Playbook

## What This Skill Does

Takes your business, your offer, and your intuition about who buys — and turns it into detailed persona profiles defined by behavior and psychology, not demographics. Each persona gets a backstory, psychographic profile, buying triggers, objections with reframes, messaging hooks mapped to their psychology, and platform-specific ad targeting criteria.

The output is a playbook you can hand to anyone writing copy, creating ads, or planning content — they'll know exactly who they're talking to, what to say, and how to reach them.

Personas are defined by stuck states and behavior patterns, not age and job title. "35-year-old PM" is a demographic. "PM who runs million-dollar roadmaps at work but can't ship their own side project because Sunday night Notion sessions have replaced actual building" is a persona.

## Phase 1: Audience Discovery

Ask these questions one at a time. Wait for the user's answer before moving to the next.

**If the user is stuck on any question:** Help them by exploring their current and past customers, the patterns they've noticed, and the language their audience uses. Most business owners know their personas intuitively — they just haven't articulated them.

### The 6 Questions

**Q1: "What do you sell, and what transformation does it deliver?"**
Not features or deliverables. The before → after. What is true about someone's life, business, or situation after working with you that wasn't true before?

*If stuck, ask:* "Think about your best customer. What was their life like before? What's it like now? That gap is the transformation."

**Q2: "Who's buying right now?"**
Describe your current customers or the people you most want to attract. Be specific: job titles, industries, income range, career stage, life situation. If you don't have customers yet, describe who you built this for.

*If stuck, ask:* "If you had to describe your last 3-5 customers (or the 3-5 people you most wish were your customers), what do they have in common? What's their job, their income range, their life stage?"

**Q3: "What are the top 3-5 pain points that drive someone to buy?"**
The real pains, not the surface ones. Not "I need more leads" but "I've been building this thing for 6 months and I'm terrified to launch because what if nobody cares." What keeps them up at night? What have they already tried?

*If stuck, ask:* "What's the conversation happening inside your ideal customer's head at 2 AM? What are they frustrated about? What have they tried that didn't work? What do they secretly fear?"

**Q4: "How many personas do you need?"**
Typically 3-5. More than 5 gets unwieldy for marketing execution. Fewer than 3 might miss important segments. If you're not sure, tell me about the different "types" of people who show interest and we'll figure out the right number.

*If stuck, ask:* "Think about the different types of people who've shown interest in what you do. Are they all the same, or are there distinct groups? Even 2-3 types is a great start."

**Q5: "What platforms will you use for ads or content?"**
Meta (Facebook/Instagram), LinkedIn, TikTok, YouTube, Google Ads? This determines the targeting criteria format. If you're not running ads yet, which platform does your audience spend the most time on?

*If stuck, ask:* "Where does your ideal customer spend time online? Are they scrolling Instagram, reading LinkedIn, watching YouTube, or somewhere else?"

**Q6: "What's your brand voice?"**
How do you communicate? Direct and no-BS? Warm and empathetic? Technical and precise? Funny and irreverent? This shapes how messaging hooks are written for each persona. Give me 3-4 adjectives or a comparison.

*If stuck, ask:* "If your brand were a person at a dinner party, how would they talk? What would they never say? Who do you sound like, and who do you definitely not sound like?"

## Phase 2: Generate the Persona Playbook

After gathering all 6 answers, generate detailed profiles for each persona, followed by cross-persona analysis.

### For Each Persona:

#### Identity Card

- **Persona Name:** Memorable and descriptive (e.g., "The Overtime Optimizer," "The Golden Handcuffs Builder," "The Perfectionist Creator")
- **One-Line Summary:** Their situation in one sentence
- **Age Range:** Typical range
- **Role/Title:** What they do professionally
- **Income Range:** What they earn
- **Company Type:** Where they work

#### Backstory & Current Situation

Write this like a character description, not a marketing brief. Include:

- What their day-to-day looks like
- What's working in their life/career (what they're good at)
- What's stuck or stalling (the gap between where they are and where they want to be)
- Their specific rituals and habits around the problem (the Sunday night Notion session, the 47-tab browser, the unfinished Shopify store)
- **The trigger event:** The specific moment they realize they need help (got passed over for promotion, spouse made a comment, saw someone less talented succeed, etc.)

#### Psychographic Profile

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Values most** | What matters to them above all else |
| **Fears most** | The worst-case scenario in their mind |
| **Believes about the problem** | Their current mental model (often wrong) |
| **Has already tried** | What solutions they've attempted and why they failed |
| **Internal narrative** | The story they tell themselves ("I should be able to figure this out alone") |
| **Identity they protect** | The part of their self-image that keeps them stuck |

#### Buying Triggers

What specific events make them ready to buy NOW:
- **External trigger:** Something happens in their environment (deadline, competitor launches, boss asks a question)
- **Internal trigger:** Emotional state shift (frustration boils over, embarrassment, fear of missing out)
- **Social trigger:** Someone else does what they've been putting off

## Rules

- **Personas must be defined by behavior and psychology, not demographics.** Age and job title are context. Stuck states, fears, beliefs, and behavioral patterns are the persona.
- **Each persona gets ONE primary pain point for ad targeting.** Don't dilute a targeted ad by addressing 3 pains. One persona, one pain, one ad.
- **Backstories must feel like real people.** "Sunday night Notion ritual," "redesigned the landing page four times," "paid for a Shopify store for 4 years without launching" — specific details that make someone say "that's me."
- **Objections must be in the persona's actual voice.** Not "I have concerns about the investment" — more like "that's a LOT of money for something I'm not sure will work."
- **Hook style mapping must match psychology.** Analytical people respond to data and myth-busting. Skeptical people respond to contrasts and questions. Aspirational people respond to stories and identity hooks. Don't assign randomly.
- **Never fabricate demographic data or market statistics.** If you don't know the exact percentage of PMs with side projects, don't invent a number.
- **Ad targeting must be actionable.** Real interest categories, real job titles, real pages they follow — not vague descriptions like "people interested in entrepreneurship."
- **If fewer than 3 distinct buyer types exist, say so.** Don't invent personas to hit a number. Two well-defined personas are better than five padded ones.
- **Messaging hooks must pass the brand voice filter.** Every hook should sound like the user's brand, not generic marketing copy. If brand-voice-router is active, apply it.
- **If humanize-ai-writing is available,** run all hooks and messaging through it. Persona playbooks that sound like AI output undermine their purpose.
- **Update, don't start from scratch.** If the user already has personas, audit and refine them rather than replacing everything.

## What This Skill Does NOT Do

- Write full ad scripts (use funnel-ad-creator for that — persona playbook feeds into it)
- Create sales page copy or email sequences
- Run ads or set up ad campaigns in Meta/LinkedIn
- Conduct primary customer research (interviews, surveys)
- Build the product or offer (use build-irresistible-offer for offer architecture)
- Design visual creative or brand assets
