# Design Launch Sequence

## What This Skill Does

Takes any offer — new or proven — and designs a complete launch sequence: the systematic, time-bound campaign that transforms a static offer into a revenue event. Uses Jeff Walker's Product Launch Formula (PLF) as the backbone, layering in Russell Brunson's Perfect Webinar Script for live events, the Seed Launch model for starting from zero, and Amy Porterfield's Evergreen Webinar framework for autopilot revenue.

This skill produces the launch architecture — the calendar, the prelaunch content outlines, the email sequence structure, the webinar skeleton, and the urgency plan. It does not write finished copy, build landing pages, or create the offer itself. Those are downstream deliverables handled by companion skills.

The output is a complete Launch Blueprint with seven sections that tell you exactly what to publish, when to publish it, what channel to use, and what each piece needs to accomplish. Whether you're doing your first Seed Launch with 50 people or running a full PLF sequence to a list of 50,000, this skill scales the framework to match your reality.

## Phase 1: Launch Discovery Questions

Ask these questions one at a time. Wait for the user's answer before moving to the next.

**If the user is stuck on any question:** Don't just repeat it. Help them think through it — suggest options based on what they've already told you, offer examples from common patterns, or reframe the question. The goal is to get a real answer, not a placeholder.

**If the user truly can't answer one** (e.g., no proof exists yet for a brand new offer), note it as a gap and keep moving. Flag it in Section 7 of the blueprint.

### The 8 Questions

**Q1: "What offer are you launching, and what transformation does it deliver?"**
Not the product name or feature list. The specific before-and-after. What is true about someone's life or business 90 days after buying that wasn't true before?

*If stuck, ask:* "What will someone's life or business look like 90 days after buying this? What will they have, know, or be able to do that they can't right now?"

**Q2: "Is this a first launch (no list, no proof) or a relaunch with an established audience?"**
This determines the entire launch model. A first launch with no list uses the Seed Launch framework. An established list with proof unlocks the full PLF sequence. Be honest — the right model depends on where you actually are, not where you wish you were.

*If stuck, ask:* "How many people are on your email list right now? Have you sold this offer (or something similar) before? Do you have any testimonials or case studies?"

**Q3: "What's your launch timeline?"**
When does cart need to open? How much runway do you have? A 2-week sprint is a different launch than a 90-day campaign. Everything in the blueprint scales to this answer.

*If stuck, ask:* "When do you want cart to open? Work backward from there. Do you have a hard deadline (cohort start, event date) or is the timeline flexible?"

**Q4: "What are the 3 false beliefs your audience holds that prevent them from buying?"**
Every prospect who doesn't buy is held back by one of three types of false beliefs. Each one becomes the backbone of your prelaunch content and webinar secrets.

- **Internal belief** (about themselves): "I'm not experienced enough," "I don't have the discipline," "I'm not technical enough"
- **External belief** (about the world): "The market is too crowded," "It's too late," "The economy is bad"
- **Vehicle belief** (about your solution): "Courses don't work for me," "I've tried coaching before," "This approach won't work for my situation"

*If stuck, ask:* "What do people say when they say NO? Those reasons are the false beliefs. What objections come up most often — about themselves, about the market, or about your specific approach?"

**Q5: "What's your origin story — the moment you discovered this solution?"**
This becomes your Epiphany Bridge — the story that takes your prospect from where they are to where you are. Not your resume. The specific moment things clicked.

*If stuck, ask:* "Think about your 'aha moment.' What were you doing before that wasn't working? What changed? What happened as a result? Tell it like you're explaining to a friend over coffee."

**Q6: "What proof do you have that this works?"**
Testimonials, case studies, your own results, credentials, data, before-and-after stories. List everything you have. For new offers with no client proof, what adjacent proof exists?

*If stuck, ask:* "Even if this is a new offer — what adjacent proof exists? Your own transformation? Related results in a different context? Credentials that transfer? A track record in a related area?"

**Q7: "What assets do you already have?"**
Email list size, social media following, existing content (blog posts, videos, podcasts), past webinars or live events, partnership connections, affiliate relationships, ad budget. This determines how much you need to build versus repurpose.

*If stuck, ask:* "What platforms do you have an audience on, even a small one? Do you have any existing content — videos, blog posts, a podcast? Do you know anyone with a complementary audience who might promote you?"

**Q8: "What's the price, and do you have a value stack already?"**
If they've used build-irresistible-offer, reference that output directly — pull the value stack, guarantee structure, and pricing. If not, get the basics: price point, what's included, any bonuses or guarantees.

*If stuck, ask:* "What are you planning to charge? Is there a fast-action bonus or early-bird price? Do you have a guarantee? If you've already built your offer with build-irresistible-offer, share that blueprint."

## Phase 2: Generate the Launch Blueprint

After gathering all 8 answers, generate a structured document with these 7 sections. Use the user's answers as raw material — don't just repeat their words back. Synthesize, sharpen, and design the actual launch architecture.

### Section 1: Launch Type Assessment

Based on Q2 (first launch vs. established) and Q7 (existing assets), recommend one of four launch models:

- **Seed Launch** — No list, no proof, starting from zero. Build a tiny list of 50-100 people, create the product WITH them through live sessions, charge a reduced price, and generate your first testimonials. Use the IKEA Effect — people value what they helped create.
- **Internal Launch** — Existing list (500+), some proof. Full PLF prelaunch sequence to your own audience. 3 PLCs over 7-10 days, followed by 4-7 day cart open.
- **Full PLF Launch** — Established list (5,000+), strong proof, possibly with affiliate/JV partners. The complete Product Launch Formula with pre-prelaunch, prelaunch, open cart, and post-launch phases.
- **Evergreen Webinar** — Proven offer with known conversion rates. Wants to move from live launches to autopilot. Registration sequence + automated webinar + post-webinar email sequence running continuously.

**Launch Readiness Scorecard:**

| Factor | Score (1-10) | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| List Size | X/10 | [Current size and quality assessment] |
| Proof Strength | X/10 | [Testimonials, case studies, results] |
| Offer Clarity | X/10 | [How well-defined is the offer and transformation] |
| Content Assets | X/10 | [Existing content that can be repurposed] |
| Timeline Feasibility | X/10 | [Is the timeline realistic for what needs to happen] |
| **Overall Readiness** | **X/10** | **[Summary recommendation]** |

If overall readiness is below 5, recommend starting with a Seed Launch regardless of ambition. Don't pretend a full PLF will work without the foundation.

### Section 2: Launch Calendar

A week-by-week (or day-by-day for launches under 3 weeks) calendar covering every phase. Format as a table:

| Date/Window | Activity | Channel | Purpose |
|-------------|----------|---------|---------|
| [Date range] | [Specific activity] | [Email, social, live, ads, etc.] | [What this accomplishes in the launch sequence] |

The calendar must include:
- **Pre-prelaunch** (2-4 weeks before PLC #1): List building, anticipation seeding, audience warming
- **Prelaunch Content delivery** (7-10 days): PLC #1, PLC #2, PLC #3 with specific dates
- **Cart open** (day 1 of open cart): The launch event — live webinar, email blast, social push
- **Mid-cart** (days 2-5): Social proof, FAQ, objection handling, case study spotlights
- **Cart close** (final 24-48 hours): Urgency escalation, last chance, countdown
- **Post-launch** (week after close): Debrief, testimonial collection, onboarding, evergreen planning

Every row should be specific enough that the user knows exactly what to create and when.

### Section 4: Email Sequence Architecture

Complete email sequence structure for every phase. For each email, provide:

| # | Phase | Subject Line | Purpose | Key Message | CTA |
|---|-------|-------------|---------|-------------|-----|
| 1 | [Phase] | [Specific subject line] | [What this email accomplishes] | [Core message in one sentence] | [What the reader should do] |

**Required phases and minimum emails:**

- **Pre-prelaunch** (2-3 emails): Seed the idea, build anticipation, gauge interest. "Something's coming" energy without being vague.
- **PLC delivery** (3 emails, one per PLC): Deliver each piece of prelaunch content. Subject lines should create curiosity, not just announce.
- **Cart open** (2-3 emails on launch day): The announcement, the value stack breakdown, the early-bird/fast-action bonus reminder.
- **Mid-cart** (3-5 emails over days 2-5): Social proof spotlight, FAQ/objection handling, case study deep dive, "behind the scenes" of the offer creation.
- **Cart close** (2-3 emails in final 24-48 hours): Urgency escalation, final testimonial, "last chance" with specific deadline, "what happens if you don't" cost-of-inaction email.
- **Post-launch** (2-3 emails): Thank you + onboarding for buyers, "doors closed" acknowledgment for non-buyers, debrief and "what's next."

Subject lines must be specific and curiosity-driven — not generic clickbait. "The 3 things I wish I knew before launching" beats "Don't miss this!"

### Section 5: Webinar/Live Event Script Skeleton

If the launch type includes a live component (Internal Launch, Full PLF, or the live version before going Evergreen), provide a complete webinar skeleton using Russell Brunson's Perfect Webinar framework.

**The ONE Thing (Big Promise):**
[State the single most compelling outcome the attendee will walk away with — or believe is possible — by the end of the webinar]

**Origin Story (Epiphany Bridge):**
- Where you were before (relatable struggle)
- The aha moment (what changed)
- Where you are now (proof it worked)
- Outline: 3-5 bullet points of the story arc

**Secret #1 — Breaks the [Internal/Vehicle/External] Belief:**
- The false belief it addresses
- The story that breaks it (Epiphany Bridge format)
- The reframe: what's actually true
- Teaching point that provides real value

**Secret #2 — Breaks the [Internal/Vehicle/External] Belief:**
- The false belief it addresses
- The story that breaks it
- The reframe
- Teaching point

**Secret #3 — Breaks the [Internal/Vehicle/External] Belief:**
- The false belief it addresses
- The story that breaks it
- The reframe
- Teaching point

**The Stack (Value Stacking Sequence):**
List every component in the order it should be revealed on slides. Each component gets its own slide. Build value progressively — the stack is the most important part of the pitch.

| Slide # | Component | Value |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| 1 | [Core offer component] | $X,XXX |
| 2 | [Next component] | $X,XXX |
| ... | ... | ... |
| N | **Total Value** | **$XX,XXX** |
| N+1 | **Your Price** | **$X,XXX** |

**Trial Close Questions (5-10 micro-yes questions to weave throughout):**
Questions designed to get the audience nodding along and mentally committing before the pitch. Example: "How many of you have ever started something and not finished it?" or "Would it be worth it if you could [outcome] in [timeframe]?"

1. [Trial close question — place after origin story]
2. [Trial close question — place after Secret #1]
3. [Trial close question — place after Secret #2]
4. [Trial close question — place after Secret #3]
5. [Trial close question — place before The Stack]
6-10. [Additional trial closes distributed throughout]

**Price Reveal Strategy:**
How to transition from the stack total to the actual price. Anchor high, reveal low, explain why.

**Closing Sequence:**
- Recap the transformation
- Restate the guarantee
- Final CTA with deadline
- Q&A framework (how to handle live questions without derailing)

**If the launch type is Seed Launch:** Replace the formal webinar with a "founding member live session" outline — less polished, more collaborative, focused on building WITH the audience rather than presenting TO them.

**If the launch type is Evergreen Webinar:** Note which elements need to be adjusted for recorded delivery (remove live-only interactions, add "watch now" urgency mechanics, design the registration-to-replay email sequence).

### Section 6: Social Proof & Urgency Plan

**Testimonial Deployment Map:**

| Asset | Where to Deploy | When |
|-------|----------------|------|
| [Specific testimonial or case study] | [Which email, PLC, or webinar moment] | [Which day of the launch] |

**Scarcity Mechanics (real only):**
- What is genuinely limited? (Spots, cohort dates, bonus availability, early-bird pricing)
- Why is it limited? (Capacity, quality, logistics)
- What happens when the limit is hit? (Price goes up, doors close, bonus disappears)

If no real scarcity exists, say so. Do not manufacture fake limits.

**Urgency Timeline:**

| Window | Mechanic | Communication |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| Cart open (first 24-48 hrs) | [Fast-action bonus, early-bird price] | [How to communicate it] |
| Mid-cart | [Social proof escalation, FAQ addressing] | [How to maintain momentum] |
| Cart close (final 24-48 hrs) | [Deadline, last chance, cost of inaction] | [How to close urgently without being sleazy] |

**Social Proof Amplification:**
How to use early buyer excitement during the cart-open period. Strategies for capturing and sharing real-time social proof (screenshots, DMs with permission, live buyer count updates).

### Section 7: Launch Gaps & Risk Assessment

Audit the entire launch plan against these criteria and flag anything missing or weak:

- **Proof gaps:** Are there claims in the PLCs or webinar without supporting evidence? Flag with [NEEDS PROOF] and recommend how to close the gap (get a testimonial, run a beta, document your own results).
- **Timeline risks:** Is the timeline realistic for the volume of content that needs to be created? If they need 15 emails, 3 videos, and a webinar in 2 weeks, flag it.
- **List size risk:** Is the list big enough for the revenue goal? Rule of thumb: 1-3% conversion rate for warm lists, 0.5-1% for cold. Calculate: list size x conversion rate x price = expected revenue. If the math doesn't work, say so.
- **Content readiness:** What needs to be created from scratch versus what can be repurposed from existing assets? Provide a creation checklist with estimated time per asset.
- **Post-launch plan:** What happens after cart closes? Is there an evergreen path? A next launch date? An upsell sequence? If nothing is planned, flag it.
- **Tech readiness:** Does the user have the email platform, landing page tool, webinar software, and payment processing needed? Don't assume — ask if unclear and flag gaps.
- **Unanswered questions:** Any questions from Phase 1 the user couldn't answer — list them here with specific recommendations for how to get the answer before launch day.

For each gap, recommend a specific, actionable step to close it — not generic advice.

## Rules

- **Never fabricate proof, testimonials, or statistics.** If proof doesn't exist, flag it as a gap. Don't invent case studies or results to fill launch content outlines.
- **Launch timelines must be realistic.** Don't compress a 30-day launch into 7 days. If the user's timeline is too aggressive for the assets needed, say so and propose alternatives (simpler launch model, fewer PLCs, text-based instead of video).
- **Every scarcity and urgency mechanic must be REAL.** Limited spots must reflect actual capacity. Deadlines must be enforced. Bonus expiration must actually happen. Manufactured urgency destroys trust with sophisticated buyers.
- **Email subject lines should be specific, not generic clickbait.** "The 3-step framework that replaced my 60-hour work weeks" beats "You won't believe this." Every subject line should pass the test: "Would I open this from someone I follow?"
- **If the user has no list and no proof, recommend Seed Launch.** Don't pretend a full PLF will work without foundation. Be honest about what's possible at each stage.
- **PLCs follow Problem-Solution-Problem structure.** Each solution seeds the next problem. PLC #1's solution creates a gap that PLC #2 addresses. PLC #2's solution creates a gap that PLC #3 addresses. PLC #3's solution creates a gap that only the full offer fills.
- **The Stack is the most important part of the webinar.** Every value component gets its own slide. Don't rush past it. The stack is where buying decisions happen.
- **If build-irresistible-offer output exists, reference it.** Pull the value stack, guarantee structure, named IP, and pricing directly. Don't rebuild what's already been designed.
- **If generate-persona-playbook output exists, use persona language.** PLCs, emails, and webinar content should speak in the language of the identified personas — their words, their pain points, their aspirations.
- **Apply brand-voice-router if active.** All content outlines and subject lines should reflect the detected brand voice.
- **Apply humanize-ai-writing to all copy output.** Subject lines, selling statements within PLCs, and any sample copy must read like a human wrote it.
- **Cart open period should be 4-7 days maximum.** Longer than 7 days kills urgency. Shorter than 4 days doesn't give enough time for decision-making. The exception is Seed Launch, where the "cart" may be a simple conversation.
- **One launch per blueprint.** If the user wants to launch multiple offers, produce separate blueprints. Don't combine launches into one document.

## What This Skill Does NOT Do

- Write the full email copy (it provides architecture and outlines, not finished emails — use build-email-story-engine for that)
- Create the landing page or sales page (use funnel-landing-page-designer)
- Build the offer itself (use build-irresistible-offer)
- Run ads or set up ad platforms
- Create the actual webinar slides or presentation
- Handle payment processing or tech setup
- Write DM scripts or sales call frameworks
- Design the post-purchase onboarding experience
