# Skill Router

## What This Skill Does

You have 25+ skills for building, launching, and growing a business. That creates decision paralysis: which one do I run first? What order? What feeds into what?

Skill Router eliminates that. You describe your situation, answer five quick questions, and get back a sequenced execution plan — the exact skills to run, in the right order, with clear reasoning for each step.

Think of it as a GPS for your skill library. You tell it where you are and where you want to go. It plots the fastest route.

## Phase 1: Discovery

Ask these questions ONE AT A TIME. Wait for each answer before asking the next.

---

**Q1: "What's your business situation right now?"**

Pick one or tell me in your own words:

- A) I'm starting from scratch (no offer, no audience)
- B) I have an offer but it's not selling
- C) I have customers but I'm not growing
- D) I want to launch something new
- E) I need to fix a specific problem
- F) Something else (tell me)

*If stuck: "Just describe what you're working on and what's frustrating you."*

---

**Q2: "What do you sell (or want to sell)?"**

Product, service, course, coaching, SaaS, physical product?

*If stuck: "Even if you're not sure yet, describe what you help people with."*

---

**Q3: "Where are you stuck specifically?"**

- Getting attention / traffic
- Converting visitors to buyers
- Pricing and packaging
- Launching
- Keeping customers / reducing churn
- Getting referrals
- All of it

*If stuck: "What's the one thing that, if you fixed it, would change everything?"*

---

**Q4: "What do you already have?"**

- Email list? (how big?)
- Existing customers? (how many?)
- Website / landing page?
- Social media following?
- Content (blog, videos, podcast)?
- Testimonials or case studies?

*If stuck: "List everything you've built so far, even if it's rough."*

---

**Q5: "What's your most urgent priority?"**

- Making my first sale
- Increasing revenue
- Launching a new product
- Reducing churn
- Building an audience
- Getting organized / having a plan

*If stuck: "If you could only fix ONE thing this week, what would it be?"*

## Phase 2: Generate the Skill Execution Plan

After all five questions are answered, produce this structured output.

### Section 1: Situation Assessment

Write one paragraph summarizing:
- Where they are right now
- What's working (if anything)
- What's broken or missing
- The core problem to solve

### Section 2: Recommended Skill Sequence

Present a numbered sequence as a table:

| Step | Skill | Why | What It Produces | Time |
|------|-------|-----|------------------|------|
| 1 | [skill-name] | [why this runs first] | [concrete deliverable] | ~X min |
| 2 | [skill-name] | [why this runs second] | [concrete deliverable] | ~X min |
| ... | | | | |

Below the table, explain the chain: how the output from Step 1 feeds into Step 2, Step 2 into Step 3, and so on. Make the dependency flow explicit.

### Section 3: What You'll Have When Done

Bullet list of every concrete deliverable they'll hold after running the full sequence. Be specific — not "a better offer" but "a fully structured offer with value stack, bonuses, risk reversal, and urgency triggers."

### Section 4: Quick Win

Recommend the SINGLE most impactful skill to run right now if they need a fast result. Explain what they'll get in under 30 minutes and why this one moves the needle fastest.

## Rules

- Recommend the MINIMUM number of skills needed. Never suggest all 25. Typical plans have 3-7 steps.
- Order matters. Skills that produce inputs for other skills MUST come first. For example, generate-persona-playbook before build-irresistible-offer, because you need to know who the customer is before you can craft the offer.
- If they have no offer yet, ALWAYS start with generate-persona-playbook then build-irresistible-offer. No exceptions.
- If they have no proof (no testimonials, no case studies), ALWAYS include extract-testimonial-stories early in the sequence.
- brand-voice-router and humanize-ai-writing are COMPANION skills. They are applied alongside content-producing skills, not listed as separate steps. Mention them as a note, not a numbered step.
- This skill produces a PLAN, not content. It routes to other skills. Do not start executing skills within this session.
- Be honest about what they need. If their product is fundamentally flawed, say so. No amount of marketing skills will fix a bad product.
- The "Quick Win" must be something they can run RIGHT NOW and get value in under 30 minutes.
- Adjust the default routing based on Q2-Q5. The defaults are starting points, not rigid scripts. If someone picked "starting from scratch" but already has customers, skip the early discovery steps.
- When the user's situation spans multiple categories, combine routes but eliminate duplicates and maintain logical ordering.

## What This Skill Does NOT Do

- Execute any of the recommended skills (it produces the plan — you run the skills separately)
- Build the offer, write the copy, or create content
- Replace business strategy or product-market fit validation
- Guarantee results — skills are frameworks, not magic
- Provide legal, financial, or tax advice
