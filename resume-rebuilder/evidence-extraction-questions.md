# Evidence Extraction Question Bank

## Step 1: Basic Context (2 Questions)

### Q1: Target Role
"What role are you targeting? Paste the job description if you have one."

### Q2: Work History Overview
"Walk me through your work history — most recent first. Job title, company, how long, one sentence on what you did."

---

## Step 2: Achievement Extraction Per Role (3-5 Questions Each)

Ask these for each role. Use the "So What?" cascade and Karissa Justice's six questions.

### Q1: Pride Point
"What's the thing you're proudest of in this role?"

**Follow-up cascade:**
- "So what? Why did that matter to the business?"
- "Can you put a number on it? Revenue, time saved, users affected, team size?"

### Q2: Visible Impact
"What did you build, fix, or change that someone else would notice?"

**Apply six questions to every answer:**
- Who was it for?
- How many people/users/customers?
- How often did it happen?
- How long did it take?
- What tools/technologies did you use?
- What's a concrete example?

### Q3: Manager's Perspective
"What would your manager say was your biggest contribution?"

### Q4: Metric Recovery (Use When User Says "I Don't Have Metrics")
"Let's find them. How big was the team? The budget? The customer base? How much time did something take before vs. after? Did anything get faster, cheaper, or bigger?"

**Additional metric-finding prompts:**
- "How many people used what you built?"
- "What was the scope — dollars, headcount, geographic reach?"
- "Did you reduce anything — time, cost, errors, complaints?"
- "Did you increase anything — revenue, adoption, satisfaction scores?"
- "What was the before state and the after state?"

### Q5: Level-Breaking Achievement (If Time Allows)
"One more — is there anything you did that was unusual for your level? Something a typical [title] wouldn't have done?"

## Non-Quantifiable Work: Alternative Categories

When achievements genuinely resist quantification, extract evidence in these four categories (via Kamara Toffolo):

1. **Innovation and Change** — Processes automated, tools adopted, workflows redesigned, new approaches introduced
2. **Influence and Strategy** — Change management led, stakeholders aligned, decisions influenced, thought leadership established
3. **Recognition** — Promotions earned (and why), awards received, invited to special projects, asked to mentor
4. **Relationships** — Trust built with key stakeholders, partnerships created, cross-functional bridges established, and the business value of each

## Metric Taxonomy (Cole Sperry Framework)

### Context Metrics (Provide Scale)
- Team size managed
- Budget owned
- Geographic scope
- Customer/user base size
- Revenue under management
- Number of direct reports

### Results Metrics (Provide Evidence of Performance)
- What improved (and by how much)
- What was saved (time, money, resources)
- What was prevented (risk, loss, downtime)
- What was created (new revenue, new capability, new process)

## Minimum Viable Bank
- 3 achievements per role
- 2-3 roles covered
- Total: 6-9 achievements

## Ideal Bank
- 5-7 achievements per role
- 3-4 roles covered
- Total: 25-30 achievements

After reaching minimum viable: "We have enough for a solid resume. Want to go deeper on any role, or should I generate?"
