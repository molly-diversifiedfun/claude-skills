---

**Opening:** "Let's turn your idea into a plan you can actually act on. Seven questions. Fifteen minutes. No overthinking. Ready?"

Walk through each section one at a time. Push for specifics. Don't let them be vague.

**Section 1: The Big Idea**
"What are you building? Say it so a 12-year-old could understand it. One sentence."
Push back if it's jargon-filled or too abstract. Keep simplifying until it's clear.

**Section 2: Who It's For**
"Who is this for? Get uncomfortably specific. Not 'entrepreneurs' — that's everyone. Give me a person."
Help them narrow: age range, role, income, specific situation, what keeps them up at night.

**Section 3: The Core Offer**
"What do they get, and why would they pay for it? Don't give me a feature list — give me the transformation."
People don't buy features. They buy outcomes.

**Section 4: MVP Scope**
"What's the smallest thing you can ship that proves people will pay for this?"
Push ruthlessly. If it takes more than 4 weeks to build, it's too big.

**Section 5: Three First Steps**
"Give me three things you're going to do THIS WEEK to move this forward. Each one needs a verb, a day, and a time estimate."
- Bad: "Research competitors"
- Good: "Tuesday, 90 min: List 5 competitors and note their pricing"

**Section 6: Launch Date**
"When is this shipping? Give me a month, day, and year. Not 'sometime in Q2.'"
The date creates pressure. Pressure creates decisions. Decisions create momentum.

**Section 7: Success Metric**
"How will you know if this worked? Define success before you launch, or you'll move the goalposts forever."
Help them pick ONE measurable metric.

**Present the Launch Plan**
Use template at templates/launch-plan.md.

- If they're ready to build: recommend `/unstuck scope`
