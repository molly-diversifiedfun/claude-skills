---

**Section 1: The trigger (Q1)**

**What we're capturing:** Why this question right now. Different triggers point to different verdicts.

"Why are you asking this question this week? Pick the ONE most-true trigger."

**The triggers + what they tend to signal:**
1. Job has become unbearable (boss, politics, scope-shift, mission-drift)
2. Side project hit a specific number (MRR / users / paying customers)
3. Runway concerns (savings burning, expenses up, partner income change)
4. Family change (kid, move, parent care, divorce)
5. Health (physical, mental, sleep, energy)
6. Boss conflict / restructure / layoff signal
7. Other (describe in one sentence)

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "Trigger 2 — side project hit $4,200 MRR last month, third month above $3K. I told myself $5K would be the trigger but I'm tired of pretending the number matters more than the pattern. Also trigger 5 — sleep is at 6hrs/night, hasn't been more than 6hrs since October."

Push back if they pick "all of them." This is a forcing function — name the ONE most-true trigger. The others get noted.

---

**Section 2: Runway (Q2)**

**What we're capturing:** Months of expenses you have saved, at current burn rate.

"How many months of full expenses do you have saved? Use your real monthly burn, not your aspirational lean-budget. Include healthcare, taxes, family obligations."

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "9 months at current burn ($8,500/mo). 14 months if I cut to lean budget ($5,500/mo — drop kid's swimming, cancel two subscriptions, no eating out, partner negotiates lower rent). I'd plan against the 9, not the 14."

Push back if they only give the lean number. Cutting to lean while quitting is a 2-variable change — high risk. Plan against real burn.

---

**Section 3: Side-project monthly net (Q3)**

**What we're capturing:** Real take-home from side project, monthly, after fees / refunds / taxes provision.

"Side-project monthly net — after Stripe fees, after refunds, after you provision for taxes (use ~30%). Three-month average if it's been variable."

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "Last 3 months: $4,200 / $3,800 / $4,400. Average $4,133. Subtract 3% fees ≈ $124. Subtract 30% tax provision ≈ $1,200. Real net: ~$2,800/month."

Push back if they quote gross revenue. Gross is fantasy money. Net is the spendable number.

---

**Section 4: Day-job monthly net (Q4)**

**What we're capturing:** Real take-home from day job. Plus benefits value if quitting means losing them.

"Day-job take-home per month, after tax. If you have healthcare, equity vest, 401k match, or other benefits you'd lose: add their monthly-equivalent value. Be honest about benefits — they're real money."

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "$11,200 take-home + ~$1,400 unvested RSUs/month + ~$800 healthcare value (the COBRA equivalent) + ~$600 401k match. Real comp value: ~$14,000/month."

Push back on a take-home-only answer if they have unvested equity or healthcare. Quitting is leaving real money on the table.

---

**Section 5: Psych temperature (Q5)**

**What we're capturing:** How burnt out you are RIGHT NOW. Not last month, not yesterday — this week.

"On a 1-10 scale: 1 = fine, energized, work-life-good. 5 = stressed but managing. 10 = can't sleep, can't focus, dread on Sunday nights, started fights at home this month. Where are you?"

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "8. Sleep at 6hrs/night for 3 months. Caught myself crying in the bathroom at work twice last month. Sunday-night dread is back. Partner mentioned twice that I'm 'not present' at dinner."

Push back on a number-without-context. The number means nothing without the behavioral signal. If they say 8 without naming what 8 looks like, ask: "What does an 8 day look like for you?"

---

**Section 6: Partner / family alignment (Q6)**

**What we're capturing:** Is the household on the same page about this decision?

"Where's your partner / family on this? Pick one: ALIGNED (we've talked, they support quitting if math works), MIXED (they support the side project but worry about the job change), NO (they don't think you should quit, period), or N/A (you're solo / no household partner decision)."

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "MIXED. Partner is happy I'm building, anxious about losing the income. We agreed 6 months ago that $5K MRR + 12 months runway would be the trigger. I'm at $4.1K + 9 months. Below both thresholds."

Push back if they answer "they support whatever I want." That's avoidance, not alignment. Partners always have an opinion. If they don't know what their partner thinks, that's information.

---

**Section 7: 6-month worst case if you QUIT (Q7)**

**What we're capturing:** The honest worst-case scenario if you pull the trigger now.

"6 months from quit-day, in the WORST realistic case (not the catastrophic case — we're not assuming a global recession; we are assuming side project growth stalls and you can't find a freelance bridge). Specific scenario. One paragraph."

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "Side project drops to $2K MRR (some churn, no new growth). Runway burns from 9 months to 4 months. I can't get a fast contract gig — market for senior PMs has been slow. I'd start looking for a full-time role at month 4, but the senior-PM job market has 6-9 month cycles right now, so I might not land before month 9. Family stress hits 8/10. Partner relationship strain. We move to my parents' for the last 3 months. Total recovery: 12 months."

Push back on "I'd be fine." That's not a worst-case, that's optimism. Make them name a specific scenario.

---

**Section 8: 6-month worst case if you DON'T quit (Q8)**

**What we're capturing:** The honest worst-case scenario if you stay.

"6 months from now if you DON'T quit, in the WORST realistic case. The continued-burnout scenario. Specific."

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "Burnout hits 10. I do mediocre work at the day job AND the side project. Day-job performance review goes from 'exceeds' to 'meets' to PIP territory. Side project growth stalls because I have no creative energy left. Partner stress hits 9. We start having the 'is this sustainable' conversation weekly. I keep the income but lose the marriage / mental health / next-level work."

Push back on "things are fine, I'd just be tired." That's not a worst-case. Make them imagine the trajectory if nothing changes for 6 months.

**Draft the verdict**

Once you have all 8 inputs, work the verdict.

**The runway math (always show this):**

```
Current burn: $[Q2 monthly]/mo
Saved: $[Q2 months × Q2 monthly] = [N] months runway at current burn

If you quit today:
- Income drops from $[Q4] day-job-comp + $[Q3] side-net = $[Q4+Q3 sum]
- To $[Q3] side-net only
- Income gap: $[Q4]/month for [how long until side net covers Q2 burn]

At current side-project growth rate of [calculated from history]:
- Side project covers full Q2 burn at month [N]
- Net runway burn until that month: [calculation]
```

**The verdict logic:**

| Triggers | Q5 | Side covers Q2 burn? | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Job unbearable AND psych ≥8 | — | Yes by month 3 | **QUIT NOW** |
| Job unbearable AND psych ≥8 | — | Yes by month 6-12 | **QUIT IN N MONTHS** (target = the month side covers burn) |
| Job unbearable AND psych ≥8 | — | No within 12 mo | **NEGOTIATE PART-TIME** (you need recovery without burning runway) |
| Side hit a number | — | Yes by month 6 | **NEGOTIATE PART-TIME** or **QUIT IN 3 MONTHS** (give yourself the soft landing) |
| Runway concerns | <7 | — | **STAY** (you need the income; build the side faster first) |
| Health | ≥9 | — | **NEGOTIATE PART-TIME** (or sabbatical, or short-term leave) |
| Family change | — | — | Case-by-case — show options A/B/C with tradeoffs |

(The matrix is a starting frame. The actual verdict reads the answer to Q1 + Q5 + (Q3 vs. Q2 burn) and synthesizes. Show confidence percentage 60-95%. Never claim 100%.)
