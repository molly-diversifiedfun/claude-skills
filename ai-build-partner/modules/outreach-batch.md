Don't ask what you can read. Draft what you can infer.

**Opening (output verbatim to the buyer):**

> "We're going to draft your 10 customer validation outreach messages. Personalized per human, asking for 15–20 minutes of their time. Each one references why YOU specifically are reaching out to THEM specifically.
>
> Normal time: 90 min of writing. With your warm list + context loaded, 5–10 minutes of editing.
>
> **Format options:** live call (most depth, most logistics overhead) or async written/voice memo (less depth, no scheduling friction). I'll draft both versions — you pick per person based on their preferred medium."

**Step 2 — Draft the batch (one outreach message per human, two formats each)**

For each person, generate TWO versions:

### Format A — Live call ask (15–20 min)

**Structure (5 lines max, ~100 words):**

1. **Acknowledge (1 line):** specific reference to their context — what makes them right for this conversation
2. **The problem you're researching (1 line):** named in their language if possible
3. **The ask (1 line):** "15 minutes on [topic] — I'm not selling anything, I'm trying to learn"
4. **Constraint (1 line):** specific time-bounded ("this week or next" — never "whenever works for you")
5. **Out (1 line):** low-pressure exit

**Example for Aamir (Datadog PM, mentioned backlog grooming pain):**

> "Aamir — quick one. You mentioned Tuesday backlog grooming a few weeks back; I'm researching how mid-level PMs at SaaS handle strategy work vs. backlog admin.
>
> Trying to validate whether there's a gap worth building something around.
>
> Would you be open to a 15-min call this week or next? Not selling anything, just trying to understand the pattern.
>
> Calendly: [link]. Or text me 3 times that work and I'll grab one. No pressure if you're slammed."

### Format B — Async voice memo / written ask (5 questions, ~5 min response)

**Structure:**

1. **Acknowledge + context (1 line)**
2. **Why I'm asking you specifically (1 line)**
3. **5 questions** — short, open-ended, no leading
4. **Format guidance (1 line):** "Voice memo or 1-line answers — whichever's faster"
5. **Out (1 line)**

**The 5 questions (customize per pain hypothesis):**

> 1. "Walk me through your worst version of [pain] in the last 30 days — what actually happened?"
> 2. "What did you try to do about it?"
> 3. "What did you wish existed that didn't?"
> 4. "Have you paid for anything to solve this? What and how much?"
> 5. "If I built [your product hypothesis in 1 line], on a scale of 1–10 how likely would you be to try it? Why?"

**Example async for Priya (Vercel PM, newsletter reply):**

> "Priya — you replied to my newsletter about strategy work a few weeks back. I'm researching the gap junior + mid PMs hit when their job becomes JIRA admin.
>
> Asking 10 people the same 5 questions — would love your take. Voice memo or 1-line answers, whichever's faster:
>
> 1. Worst version of backlog-admin-mode in your last 30 days?
> 2. What did you try?
> 3. What did you wish existed?
> 4. Have you paid for anything to solve this? What/how much?
> 5. If a 5-week paid cohort taught real strategy doc work + got you EM buy-in, 1–10 likely to try? Why?
>
> No deadline pressure — but if you can in the next week, that helps me decide what to build."
