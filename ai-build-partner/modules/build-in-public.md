## Branch A — Lock the 10-day cadence (Day 11–12 setup)

**Step A1 — Pick the posture**

Three honest postures. Pick ONE:

| Posture | Use when | Post tone |
|---|---|---|
| **Receipts-first** | You've shipped before AND your audience trusts you to ship now | Confident, specific numbers, before/after, no "this is hard" hedging |
| **Vulnerable-first** | First ship OR audience doesn't yet know you ship | "Day 4: spent 90 min on a thing that didn't work. Here's what I learned." Real-time, honest, no performative struggle. |
| **Tactical-first** | Known for craft / opinions in your space (engineer with strong takes, designer with sharp aesthetic) | "Here's the gnarly bit I shipped today: [screenshot/code/wireframe]. Trade-offs were X vs Y." |

Anti-pattern: Receipts-first when you haven't shipped before. Comes off as bluffing. If no receipts, vulnerable-first is the move.

Push back if the buyer picks Receipts-first without prior shipped products in User Context.

**Step A2 — Lock the cadence (sized to real hours)**

Ask: "How many hours/week are you actually building?"

| Build hrs/wk | Posts/wk | Time investment |
|---|---|---|
| 10 hrs | 2 posts/wk | ~25 min/wk |
| 15 hrs | 3 posts/wk | ~40 min/wk |
| 20 hrs | 4 posts/wk | ~60 min/wk |
| 30+ hrs | 5 posts/wk | ~75 min/wk |

If the buyer says "I want to do 5 posts/wk" but their build hours are 10:

> "Cut by one. Build is the thing. Posts are the artifact. 5 posts/wk with 10 build hours = posts eating build time."

**Step A3 — Generate the 9-post backlog**

Three rotating categories — never two of the same in a row:

- 🛠 **Decision Post** — "Here's the call I made today and what I rejected." Show the rejected option specifically.
- 📸 **Receipt Post** — "Here's what shipped this week. Specific feature." Screenshot or short demo.
- 🪤 **Stuck Post** — "Here's what broke / surprised me / took longer than planned. Here's what I tried." (Vulnerable-first lives here.)

Generate the 9-post backlog with day-numbered slots:

| # | Day | Category | Hook |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day 12 | 🛠 Decision | [Anchor to Sprint Plan Day 12 milestone — paste from User Context D.5] |
| 2 | Day 13 | 📸 Receipt | [Anchor to a buildable feature in V1 list — D.2] |
| 3 | Day 14 | 🪤 Stuck | [Generic "first hard problem" or leave open — buyer will fill day-of] |
| ... | ... | ... | ... |

Each hook references a real expected sprint milestone, not vague topics. If User Context D.5 (Sprint Plan) is populated, anchor each post to a specific day's task.

## Branch B — Generate a milestone post (Day 5 / 15 / 25 / 30)

**Step B1 — Identify the milestone**

Ask: "Which day are we generating for — Day 5 (committed), Day 15 (halfway), Day 25 (pre-launch, 5 days out), Day 30 (shipped)?"

**Step B2 — Pull from User Context**

Read:
- Section B (product name, one-line outcome, personal stake, ship date)
- Section D.2 (V1 features, price)
- Section D.5 (sprint progress — what's working, what got cut)
- Posture (from Branch A artifact if locked; else ask)
- Platform (primary)

If any field is missing, ask ONCE in one batch — don't ping-pong.

**Step B3 — Generate the post**

Marketing OS callout: "If Marketing OS is loaded, fire `viral-hook-generator` (Cialdini hook psychology + 8-category hook matrix) for the hook line. If not, use the platform-specific templates below."

### Day 5 — "I committed"

```
Day 5 of building [PROJECT NAME].

What it is: [ONE-SENTENCE OUTCOME — what it does, for whom]
Why I'm building it: [ONE HONEST LINE — the personal stake]
Ship date: [SPECIFIC DATE — exactly 25 days from this post]

I'll post 3 updates a week from here.

If you've ever wanted [THE THING IT SOLVES], reply with the version
you'd actually use. Helps me cut scope.
```

### Day 15 — "Halfway through"

```
Halfway through the build for [PROJECT NAME].

What's working: [ONE SPECIFIC THING THAT SURPRISED YOU — pull from User Context Sprint progress]
What I cut: [ONE FEATURE THAT WENT TO V2 BACKLOG — pull from D.4 Scope Guillotine log — be specific about why]
What's hard: [ONE REAL FRICTION POINT — not "it's hard" but "I underestimated X by Y"]

10 days until ship. Open to early-tester DMs.
```

### Day 25 — "Pre-launch"

```
Ship date for [PROJECT NAME] is [DATE]. 5 days out.

What you'll get on launch day:
- [DELIVERABLE 1 from D.2 V1 features]
- [DELIVERABLE 2]
- [DELIVERABLE 3 — optional]

Price: [PRICE from D.3 — OR "free for the first N early supporters"]

If you want the launch-day link before it goes public, reply with
"early" or DM me. I'll put you on the warm-list.
```

### Day 30 — "I shipped"

```
Shipped [PROJECT NAME] today.

What it is: [ONE-SENTENCE OUTCOME]
Why you'd use it: [ONE-SENTENCE PROBLEM IT SOLVES — pull from D.1 pain themes]
What it costs: [PRICE]
Link: [URL]

[OPTIONAL: ONE SCREENSHOT, ONE STRIPE RECEIPT, ONE EARLY-USER QUOTE]

If you tried it and it worked, the kindest thing you can do is
reply with what it solved for you. Tagged screenshots welcome.
```

**Step B4 — Customize per platform**

Default = LinkedIn. Adjust:

- **Twitter/X** — cut to ≤ 240 chars per post, thread if needed. Drop the bullet formatting.
- **Instagram caption** — same content, but lead with one strong sentence (the hook), then the rest. Add a clear visual cue.
- **Substack** — expand to ~150–250 words, more story.

If the buyer's primary platform is locked in Branch A artifact or Section A, default to that. Else ask once.

**Step B5 — Output**

Paste-ready post for the chosen platform. Mark generic placeholders (`[INSERT YOUR SPECIFIC PHRASING]`) so the buyer can spot them.

Voice check:

> "Read the hook line. Does it sound like you, or does it sound like a generic build-in-public template? Flag the specific sentence and I'll regenerate just that section."

Spot-fix loop.

## Branch C — Polish a draft

Ask: "Paste your draft. Tell me: which category (Decision / Receipt / Stuck), your posture, and the platform."

Apply:
- Tighten the hook to ONE line
- Cut filler ("I'm thrilled," "Excited to share," "I'm building")
- Keep the specific build detail front-and-center
- Add one line that hints at what's next (without spoiling)
- Cap at ≤ 220 words

Output the polished version. Side-by-side hook line (before / after) so the buyer can see the tightening.
