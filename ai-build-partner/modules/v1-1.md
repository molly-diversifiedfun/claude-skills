**Step 0 — Check User Context first (Mode 1 behavior)**

This skill picks THE ONE V1.1 ship from a candidate list of 5–15 items. Day 38–60 of the iteration cycle. Before the Opening, scan the user's uploaded User Context file:

- **Section D — Phase 7** (T17 PMF Scorecard): if PMF verdict was ITERATE, the failing signals are the iteration target.
- **Section D.1** (T04 Validation): customer pain themes — V1.1 candidates should map back to these.
- **Section D.4** (T07 Scope Guillotine log): items that got parked to V2 during the build. These are V1.1 candidates by default.
- **Section D.12** (T15 Weekly Ship Check): recurring symptoms from Sunday reviews.

If the buyer hasn't run `/unstuck pmf` yet at Day 60, ask whether they want to score PMF first (they should) or push ahead because they're mid-cycle (Day 38). Default = push ahead at Day 38, route to `/unstuck pmf` at Day 60.

Don't ask what you can read. Pre-build the candidate list from the User Context before asking.

---

**Opening (output verbatim to the buyer):**

> "You have 10 'I should fix this' items rotating in your head. You have one to two weeks of margin time. V1.1 ships ONE thing. Not three. Not two. ONE.
>
> We're going to:
> 1. **Dump your candidate list** (5–15 items, no judgment)
> 2. **Score each on 4 dimensions** (Retention impact · WTP impact · Build effort · Scope risk)
> 3. **Apply the formula** (R × W) × (BE × SR) ÷ 25 — the math, not your gut
> 4. **Pick THE ONE** — and force every unselected candidate into a bucket (V2 / killed / already covered)
>
> **One ground rule:** you will be tempted to bundle. Bundling V1.1 → V1.2 → V1.3 into one ship is the same scope-creep disease the Scope Guillotine fixed at Day 9. Hard cap: ONE ship. I'm going to enforce it.
>
> Ready?"

---

**Step 1 — Dump the candidate list**

Ask:

> "Write down EVERY 'I should fix this' / 'I should add that' item rotating in your head. Customer-feedback items, your-own-observation items, things you're embarrassed about, the feature your loudest user keeps DM-ing you about. Aim for 5–15 candidates. If you have more than 15, you're stalling — pick the 15 most-rotating."

If User Context has populated V2 backlog (D.4) + Weekly Ship Check entries (D.12) + PMF failing-signal notes (Phase 7) — pre-fill the list from there. Ask the buyer to add anything missing.

Output the locked candidate list as a numbered table:

| # | Candidate | Source (customer / me / metric) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | ... | ... |

---

**Step 2 — Score each candidate on 4 dimensions**

Walk one candidate at a time. For each:

| Dimension | What it asks | 1 = | 5 = |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Retention impact** | If I ship this, do retained users come back more? | No measurable retention effect | Direct retention lift (fixes a Day-7 churn driver) |
| **WTP impact** | If I ship this, do customers pay more / would non-buyers buy? | No price/conversion effect | Direct WTP lift (unblocks a "would buy if" objection) |
| **Build effort** | How much margin time to ship this? | 2+ weeks | < 1 day |
| **Scope risk** | How likely is this to expand mid-build? | High — touches 3+ surfaces | Low — single-file change, isolated |

Note: Build Effort and Scope Risk are *inverted* (5 = low/easy/contained). The formula treats them as quality multipliers — high = cheap and contained.

Score one candidate at a time. After each, push back on generous scores:

> "You scored Build Effort a 5 (under 1 day). Walk me through what 'shipped' means — including ESP wiring, customer comms, edge cases. If it's actually 2–3 days, score it a 4."

Builders consistently over-estimate their own speed and under-weight scope creep. Push on both, every time.

---

**Step 3 — Apply the formula**

> Score = (Retention × WTP) × (Build Effort × Scope Risk) ÷ 25

Multiplication, not addition, is load-bearing. An item that's high impact but a 2-week project ranks lower than a medium-impact item shippable in a day. The math punishes scope ballooning on purpose.

Output the scored table:

| # | Candidate | R | W | BE | SR | Score |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

Rank by score, descending.

---

**Step 4 — Pick THE ONE**

Highest score wins. Tiebreak: highest Build Effort (most shippable). Speed beats theoretical impact during iteration.

> "Your V1.1 ship is: **[paste].** Score: [N]. Build effort: [≤ N days]. Scope risk: contained."

If the buyer pushes back ("but I really want to do candidate 3 instead, even though it scored lower") — push back harder:

> "Why? Walk me through the score gap. If candidate 3 is actually higher impact than its score suggests, what dimension did we under-score? Re-score that dimension; don't override the math."

Only allow override if the buyer can name the specific dimension that was mis-scored. Otherwise the formula stands.

Validate against the Hard Cap test:

- ☐ Single, contained, shippable in 1-2 weeks?
- ☐ Touches ≤ 2 surfaces?
- ☐ Buyer can describe "done" in one sentence?

If any check fails → re-scope to a smaller V1.1 ship. Don't ship if it can't pass.

---

**Step 5 — Park the rest**

Every unselected candidate goes into one of three buckets. Force the bucket — this is the step builders skip, and then the same items rotate back next cycle.

> "For each unselected candidate, pick a bucket:
> - **V2 backlog** — right idea, wrong cycle. Re-score in V1.2 cycle.
> - **Permanently killed** — wrong feature. Write the reason (one sentence). It doesn't come back.
> - **Already covered** — realized while scoring it's actually shipped or addressed elsewhere. Remove from rotation."

Output the parked-candidate table:

| # | Candidate | Bucket | Reason |
|---|---|---|---|

If the buyer leaves anything unbucketed, push back:

> "Candidate 4 doesn't have a bucket. It can't stay in 'maybe' — that's how it rotates back next cycle. V2 backlog (re-score in V1.2), killed (won't ship, name the reason), or already covered. Pick one."

---

**Step 6 — Flag architecture decisions**

If any candidate is "split this into a new product" or "rebuild this on a different stack" — flag it for `/unstuck scaling-lever`, not V1.1.

> "Candidate [N] reads as a strategy decision, not a feature decision. T18 / `/unstuck v1.1` doesn't score architecture. Move it to a separate decision — run `/unstuck scaling-lever` for product-shape choices, or stage a `/unstuck discovery` if it's a real pivot. Don't try to bundle it into V1.1."
