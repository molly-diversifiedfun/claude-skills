**Section 1: Starting point (Q1)**

**What we're capturing:** Where you are on the audience axis, calibrated.

"What's your current following on any single platform? Not 'I'm starting from scratch' — pick the platform with the most and give me the number."

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "LinkedIn: 1,200 connections, but I haven't posted in 3 years. Twitter: 84 followers, dormant since 2021. Newsletter: 12 people, all friends. So functionally near-zero."

The 5/5 says the real number AND the engagement state. A buyer who says "I have 1,200 LinkedIn" without admitting it's dormant is going to set Q7 too high. Push back on a single-number answer without context.

---

**Section 2: Domain / topic (Q2)**

**What we're capturing:** The thing you'd build the audience around. One sentence.

"What would you build the audience around? A topic, a problem, a category. One sentence."

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "Side-project shippers — senior tech employees with stalled side projects, the gap between how they help their team and how they treat their own work."

Push back if it sounds like a brochure ("personal development thought leadership"). Real topics are specific enough that a 12-year-old can picture the human being helped.

---

**Section 3: Persona (Q3)**

**What we're capturing:** Who you're talking to — one specific human, behaviorally specific.

"Who's the very first person you want to follow you? Get uncomfortably specific."

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "Aamir, 26, PM at a B2B observability SaaS, 18 months in. He's been telling himself 'this weekend I'll start the newsletter' for 6 months. Last Saturday he opened his Substack draft, closed it, and watched a 90-min YouTube video about productivity instead."

The 5/5 has: name, age, role, company type, behavioral specifics, the exact-moment-of-stuck. Push back if any slot is missing.

---

**Section 4: Unique angle (Q4)**

**What we're capturing:** Your position vs. existing voices in the space.

"Who else is talking to [Q3 persona]? What's your position vs. them — in one sentence?"

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "Lenny's Newsletter covers product strategy at scale. Reforge covers PM career growth. Nobody is talking to senior PMs about their SIDE projects — the bench of stalled stuff outside their day job. That's my lane."

Push back on "I have a unique perspective." That's a non-answer. Specifics: who else is in the lane, where they ARE talking, where the gap is.

---

**Section 5: Time per week (Q5)**

**What we're capturing:** Hours you actually have, not aspirational hours.

"How many hours per week — REAL hours, not aspirational — do you have for audience-building? 3, 5, or 10?"

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "5 hours, broken into Tues 60min, Thurs 60min, Sat 3hrs. That's the realistic envelope around my day job and parenting Saturday morning."

Push back on 10 hours from a buyer with a full-time job + family. 10 hrs/week of writing is a part-time second job. Realistic for most senior employees is 3-5 hrs.

---

**Section 6: Platform pick (Q6)**

**What we're capturing:** ONE primary platform. Repurposing is downstream — don't try to multi-home from week 1.

"Which platform are you posting to as the primary? LinkedIn / Twitter / Substack / Instagram / YouTube. Pick ONE. Everything else is repurposing later."

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "LinkedIn primary. I'm a senior tech employee, my audience lives there, and I have 1,200 dormant 1st-degree connections to reactivate. Substack secondary for monthly long-form. Twitter and IG dormant for now."

Push back on "all of them at once." That's how people burn out at week 3. One primary, one secondary at most.

**Platform fit cheat-sheet (offer if they say "I don't know which"):**
- LinkedIn → senior tech employees, B2B, professional audience, dormant 1st-degree to reactivate
- Substack → essayist voice, long-form, lower-volume higher-loyalty
- Twitter / X → fast feedback, public-square posture, dev/founder audiences
- Instagram → consumer / lifestyle / creator product, visual-first
- YouTube → educational, evergreen, willing to do video reps

---

**Section 7: 30-day proof goal (Q7)**

**What we're capturing:** A specific number that says "audience is real enough to launch into."

"What number does Day 30 need to hit for you to feel like you have an audience to launch into? Specifics — followers count, email subs, DMs, replies. Pick one or two."

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "200 net-new LinkedIn followers + 50 newsletter subs + at least 3 inbound DMs from strangers asking thoughtful questions. If I don't hit those, I extend or I rescope."

Push back if they pick a vanity number ("1,000 followers") with no engagement signal. Followers without DMs/replies/subs = nothing to launch into. The signal numbers are: inbound DMs, newsletter conversions, replies to your posts.

**The hard truth:** if Q1 was near-zero and Q5 is 3 hrs/week, Q7 of "1,000 followers in 30 days" is fantasy. The skill will sanity-check this in the output.

---

**Section 8: Dormant audience anywhere? (Q8)**

**What we're capturing:** Sleeping audiences you can reactivate — usually the fastest 30-day lift.

"Do you have ANY sleeping audience anywhere? LinkedIn 1st-degree connections that count even if you haven't posted. An old Twitter. A newsletter you let die. A Slack community you're already in. People in your phone you used to work with."

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "LinkedIn: 1,200 1st-degree, mostly former coworkers from my Heap years, very high relevance to the audience. Twitter: 84 dormant, low relevance. A Discord I built in 2022, ~30 active members but it's a different topic. So real asset: LinkedIn 1st-degree."

Push back on "no, I'm starting from zero." Almost everyone has dormant audience somewhere. Even 80 LinkedIn connections is enough to seed a reactivation post that gets 200 net-new in 30 days.

**Draft the bundle**

Once you have all 8 inputs (or have inferred Q2-Q4 from context + got Q1/Q5-Q8 from buyer), generate the artifact at `templates/audience-from-zero.md` shape.

**Output rules — load-bearing:**

### 30-day cadence sized to Q5

| Q5 (hrs/week) | Daily/weekly schedule |
|---|---|
| 3 hrs | 2 LinkedIn posts/wk (60 min each, Tue + Thu) + 30 min/wk reactive commenting on other people's posts |
| 5 hrs | 3 LinkedIn posts/wk + 1 long-form Substack/wk (or 1 Reels/wk if Q6 = IG) + 30 min reactive comments |
| 10 hrs | 3 LinkedIn + 1 Substack + 1 video/Reel + 60 min reactive comments + 60 min DM 1:1 outreach to 5 people/wk |

**Anti-pattern:** Buyer wants the 10-hr schedule with 5 hrs available. Refuse. Pick 5-hr schedule and note the upgrade path at week 4 if they want to expand. Sustainable wins.

### Topic clusters

Generate 3-5 topic themes the buyer rotates through. Pull from Q2 + Q4. Example output for the side-project-shipper niche:
1. **Identity mirror** — the gap between how senior employees help their team vs. their own projects
2. **Frameworks** — proprietary or named methods (Scope Guillotine, 70% Rule)
3. **Vulnerability** — your own stalled projects, your own near-quits
4. **Proof** — receipts from people who shipped (yours + others, with permission)
5. **Counter-narrative** — what the industry tells you (10x! grind!) vs. what actually works

Each cluster gets 6-7 posts over 30 days. Rotate the cluster — never two of the same in a row.

### First 10 posts — hooks pre-written

For each, format:

> **Post #N — [Cluster] — Day [X]**
> **Hook line:** [actual paste-ready first sentence]
> **Body shape:** [2-3 line description of what to write]
> **CTA:** [what to ask for at the end]
> **Engagement asset:** [if applicable — a graphic, a poll, a question]

Use Q3 (persona) + Q4 (angle) to generate hooks. Real hook examples (Molly's voice):

> Post #1 — Identity mirror — Day 1
> Hook: "I noticed something about senior PMs at FAANGs. They give their team the strategy framework, the timeline, the priority filter. Then they ghost their own side project for the 4th weekend in a row."
> Body shape: name the gap, give a specific example, ask the persona which side of the gap they're on
> CTA: "Which one are you? Comment 'team' or 'me'"

If Q4 is empty or generic, the skill drops to 5 posts instead of 10 and flags Q4 as "needs more specificity before launching."

### 30-day scope sanity check

Run the math: Q1 starting + Q5 hours × 4 weeks → realistic Q7 outcome at typical conversion (LinkedIn: 1% follower-to-engagement, 0.2% post-impression-to-follower, etc.).

If Q7 > realistic outcome by >2x, output a recalibration:

> **Reality check:** Your Q7 target ([number]) requires [X] LinkedIn posts × [Y] avg engagement. At Q5 = [Z] hrs/week, you produce [N] posts. Math says you'll hit [N × engagement] — about [%] of your target.
>
> **Two options:**
> 1. Adjust Q7 to [realistic number] — same hours, achievable target
> 2. Extend the window to [60 days] — same target, 2x the time
>
> Don't increase Q5 — those hours don't exist. Don't keep Q7 at fantasy — you'll quit at week 3.

### Dormant-audience activation script

If Q8 named anything, generate the 3-step reactivation:

1. **The re-intro post** (Day 1): A LinkedIn post or Substack-relaunch that names the gap (3 years off, why, what changed). Show vulnerability + the angle.
2. **The 1st-degree DM** (Days 2-7): A message to 10 of your highest-relevance dormant connections — "Hey, I'm restarting writing here. Topic is [Q2]. Would love to know if this is useful to you / what would actually be useful. No pitch — just want to know if I'm aiming at the right thing."
3. **The Slack/community engagement** (Days 5-14): If Q8 named a Slack community, drop value in 5 threads over 2 weeks. No pitch. Just be useful in the spaces you're already in.

### Day 30 readiness gate

Output a clear branch:

> **At Day 30, look at Q7's number:**
> - **Hit Q7 → GO LAUNCH.** Audience is real enough. Validate by DM-ing 10 followers about your side-project idea. Their replies are validation. Skip /unstuck validate. Run /unstuck scope next.
> - **60-99% of Q7 → EXTEND 15 DAYS.** You're on pace, just slow. Same cadence, 15 more days. Don't change strategy.
> - **Under 60% of Q7 → REASSESS.** Something is wrong. Either Q4 angle is weak (your hook isn't differentiated) or Q6 platform fit is wrong. Run /unstuck audience-from-zero again with the post-mortem data.
