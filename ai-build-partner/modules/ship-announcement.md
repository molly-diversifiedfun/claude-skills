---

**Section 1: Product name (Q1)**

**What we're capturing:** The canonical name as it appears on your landing page.

"What's the product called? Exactly as it appears on the landing page — capitalization, punctuation, the whole thing."

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "Outli.ne — lowercase, with the period before 'ne'. The portfolio builder."

Push back if they give a tagline instead of the name. Name first.

---

**Section 2: One-line description (Q2)**

**What we're capturing:** What the product is in one sentence a 12-year-old could understand.

"Describe the product in one sentence. No jargon. A 12-year-old should know what it does."

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "A portfolio builder that lets visual creators ship a real site in one afternoon without writing code or paying a designer."

Push back on:
- "It's a platform that…" (platform is a non-word — what does it actually DO?)
- "We help [audience] [outcome] via [technology]" (corporate jargon — drop "we help," drop "via")
- "It's like X but for Y" (acceptable if X is universally known; otherwise be specific)

---

**Section 3: Who it's for (Q3)**

**What we're capturing:** The persona — one specific human, behavioral-specific.

"Who's the very first person you want to read this announcement and click through? Get uncomfortably specific."

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "Vanessa, 31, illustrator three years freelance. She's been telling herself she'll build a portfolio site 'this weekend' for nine months. Has Squarespace tabs open. Last Tuesday she said 'I'd pay someone to just decide for me' in a Discord group."

The 5/5 includes: name, role, specific behavior, the exact-moment-of-stuck. Push back if any slot is missing.

---

**Section 4: The problem it solves (Q4)**

**What we're capturing:** The pain point in their words, not yours.

"What's the specific pain this kills? In their words, not marketing-speak. The thing they'd say to a friend at coffee."

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "Every portfolio builder for visual creators wants you to fill in a 12-step wizard. Visual creators don't think in wizards. They think 'show my work, look good, send it to clients today.' We make THAT the flow."

Push back if it sounds like a brochure. Ask: "Would the persona actually say this out loud?"

---

**Section 5: The link (Q5)**

**What we're capturing:** The landing page URL.

"What's the URL you want people to land on?"

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "outli.ne — the main landing page. Not the signup page. Let them see the work first."

Push back if they give a Notion link or a personal LinkedIn — the announcement should drive to a public product surface.

---

**Section 6: Ship date (Q6)**

**What we're capturing:** A specific calendar date.

"When did it ship? Or when does it ship? Month, day, year."

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "Tuesday May 13, 2026. Live since 9am Pacific."

Used in the SHIPPED stamp graphic + the /shipped mailto. Don't accept 'today' or 'this week' — pick a date.

---

**Section 7: One vulnerable beat (Q7) — optional but encouraged**

**What we're capturing:** The behind-the-scenes specific that makes the announcement different from every "I'm thrilled to share" post.

"This is the bit that separates your post from every corporate launch announcement. One vulnerable beat — a setback you hit during the build, a moment you almost killed it, the personal reason you built it, the embarrassing first-draft thing. ONE specific moment, not a paragraph of feelings."

**5 examples of 5/5 answers — pattern-match to one that's close:**

> 1. **The setback:** "I scoped this three times. The first scope was a 14-person co-founder team. The second was an LLC. The third was 'me, alone, six weeks, ship the thing.' Two earlier versions are sitting dead in a Notion archive."

> 2. **The almost-killed-it:** "On Day 18 I sent the kill email to my partner. Drafted, didn't send. Re-read the original JTBD doc, realized I'd built up to it sideways instead of straight at it. Cut three features, shipped Day 22."

> 3. **The personal reason:** "Built this because I lost $200 a month for two years to gift cards I forgot to use. The product is literally me trying to fix my own bug."

> 4. **The embarrassing first draft:** "The first version had a 23-field onboarding form. I showed it to my partner. He laughed for thirty seconds straight. Shipped with 3 fields."

> 5. **The behind-the-scenes math:** "Built this in 10-hour weeks while still consulting full-time. 60 hours total, spread across 6 weekends. Math says I'm hourly-rate-negative for the next 8 months. I shipped anyway."

If they say "I don't have one":
- Show the 5 examples again
- Suggest: "Tell me about the moment you almost didn't ship. There's always one. The first thing that comes to mind, even if it feels boring."
- If they still pass: "OK — we'll write the announcement without it. The IG and Twitter versions take the hit (~30% less engagement on launch posts without a vulnerable hook in my data set), the LinkedIn and Substack will still work because the structural narrative carries them."

**Skip note:** Q7 is optional. If user types `skip`, mark it and move on. Don't gatekeep.

**Section 8: Which platforms (Q8)**

**What we're capturing:** Which of IG / LinkedIn / Twitter / Substack the buyer wants drafts for.

"Which platforms are you posting to? Default is all 4 (IG, LinkedIn, Twitter, Substack). If you only have 2 active platforms, just tell me which — I won't waste time drafting for places you don't post."

**Example of a 5/5 answer:**
> "LinkedIn and Substack. I'm dormant on IG and don't tweet."

Default to all 4 if they say "all" or don't answer specifically.

**Draft the bundle**

Once you have all 8 inputs (or have inferred 6 from context + got Q7 + Q8 from the buyer), generate the artifact at `templates/ship-announcement.md` shape.

**Voice rules across all 4 platform variants — load-bearing:**

- **Never lead with the product reveal.** Lead with the vulnerable beat, a specific number, or the moment-of-pain. The product appears in lines 4-7, not line 1.
- **Banned openers** (do not generate, do not pass through user input): "I'm thrilled to announce," "Excited to share," "Introducing," "After months of hard work," "Big news," "It's official," "The day has finally come," "I'm proud to announce."
- **Banned phrases:** unlock, unleash, manifest, journey, transformation, level up, game-changer, revolutionary, synergy, holistic, paradigm shift, deep dive (as verb), lean in, circle back, move the needle, at the end of the day.
- **Banned number:** 47. If a number is needed, use the actual ship number or a different round number.
- **First-person, specific, vulnerable.** Match Molly's voice from `references/core.md`. Smart-friend-two-drinks-in tone.

**Platform-specific generation rules:**

### Instagram caption (150-220 words)
- **Hook line:** Use Q7 (vulnerable beat) or a specific number from Q4/Q6 as line 1. If Q7 was skipped, use a specific number or behavioral observation from Q3/Q4.
- **Body:** 2-3 short paragraphs. Vulnerable beat → what shipped → who it's for.
- **CTA:** "Link in bio" or "DM me '[KEYWORD]'" or "Comment 'ship' and I'll send the link" — pick one.
- **Hashtags:** `#ShipItWall` is mandatory. Add 4-7 contextual hashtags based on product type (B2B SaaS: `#buildinpublic #indiehackers #startuplife`. Consumer/creator: `#sidehustle #creator #shipit`. Course/info: `#cohort #onlinecourse`. Match the actual audience.)
- **Alt-text:** One sentence describing the SHIPPED-stamp image generated in section 1 of the artifact. Format: "Hand-drawn pink-and-mauve sketch of a small character stamping SHIPPED across a [product type], with the product name and ship date hand-lettered below."

### LinkedIn post (250-400 words)
- **Open:** With the specific moment-of-pain from Q4, told as a scene. Not as a category. Example: "Tuesday afternoon. Vanessa is in her ninth month of telling herself she'll build a portfolio site 'this weekend.' She has Squarespace tabs open. Last Tuesday she told a Discord 'I'd pay someone to just decide for me.'"
- **Pivot to product:** "That's who I built [PRODUCT NAME] for."
- **What it is:** ONE sentence — Q2.
- **What's different:** ONE specific thing. Not three. Q4's pain → product's mechanism.
- **The vulnerable beat:** Q7, told as one specific moment.
- **Soft CTA:** "DM me if you're a [persona]" or "Comment if you want the link" or "Forward this to the person you know who's been stuck on [thing]."
- **Link at the bottom**, not the top. LinkedIn rewards comments before links.

### Twitter — single tweet (280 chars max)
- **Format:** `[HOOK]. [LINK]. #ShipItWall`
- **Hook examples (pattern-match Q7 + Q4):**
  - "Built [PRODUCT] in 6 weeks because every [category] for [persona] was a 12-step wizard. [LINK]. #ShipItWall"
  - "Day 18 I almost killed [PRODUCT]. Day 22 I shipped it. Live now → [LINK]. #ShipItWall"
- **Test:** Does the tweet make sense without context? If you need to read the next tweet to get the hook, it's a thread tweet, not a single tweet.

### Twitter — 5-tweet thread
- **1/** Hook — Q7 vulnerable beat or specific number. End with "↓"
- **2/** The problem — Q4 in the persona's words. End with "↓"
- **3/** What we built — Q2 + Q5 + ONE specific differentiator. End with "↓"
- **4/** The vulnerable beat — Q7 if not used in 1/, otherwise expand on it. End with "↓"
- **5/** Link + CTA. "Live now → [LINK]. DM 'ship' if you want me to walk you through it. #ShipItWall"
- **No subtweets.** No screenshots. Plain text only.

### Substack / Newsletter (600-900 words)
- **Open:** Story-led. The moment you decided to build this — with a real scene, real details, real time-of-day. Example: "It was a Tuesday in October. I was on the subway home from a day-job meeting where I'd just spent forty minutes explaining to a stakeholder why our portfolio site wasn't 'updated this quarter.' On the way home I opened my own portfolio. Last updated 2022. I closed it. Then I opened a Notion doc and started writing what would become [PRODUCT NAME]."
- **The problem:** Q4 in 2-3 paragraphs. Tell stories of specific people you talked to. Pull from your User Context Section D.5 (10 conversations) if populated.
- **The build itself:** 1-2 specific setbacks. Q7's vulnerable beat goes here, expanded. Include at least one moment you almost killed it.
- **What shipped:** Q2. ONE paragraph. Don't list features.
- **Who it's for:** Q3 — one specific persona, by name and behavior.
- **The ask:** "If this is for you, [LINK]. If it's for someone you know, forward this. If you want to tell me why you almost killed your last project, hit reply — I read everything."
- **Attribution:** If they used the Ship It System, end with: "Built with the Ship It System — theshipitsystem.com."
