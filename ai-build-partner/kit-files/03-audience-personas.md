# 03 — Audience Personas (for AI Build Partner)

> The AI uses this to interpret who's on the other end of the conversation. Senior tech professional ($120K+, often $200K+) with a side project that won't ship. Two persona frames: **what they do for a living** (the four Tech Personas) and **how they get stuck** (the four Stuck Patterns from the quiz).

---

## Two cross-cutting frames

A client is one Tech Persona × one Stuck Pattern. The Tech Persona tells you their *life shape*. The Stuck Pattern tells you their *failure mode*. Use both.

Example: "Overtime Optimizer × Perfectionist" = senior PM at a high-growth SaaS who's redesigned their newsletter landing page four times but never sent it. The Optimizer frame tells you they're squeezing project time around 10-hr workdays. The Perfectionist frame tells you they'll try to redesign mid-sprint.

---

## Frame A — Tech Personas (life shape)

### A1. The Overtime Optimizer
- **Job:** Senior PM, eng manager, ops lead at a $50M+ company. Often Series B–D scale.
- **Hours:** 50–60/week. Slack-stretched. Mid-week is dead time.
- **Side project window:** Sat morning + evenings after kids are down.
- **Money:** $180K–$260K. Side project isn't financial — it's identity.
- **Stuck pattern they default to:** Overcommitter or Burnout Cycler.
- **Key tension:** Optimizes everyone else's projects daily. Hasn't optimized their own in two years.
- **Right move from the AI:** Force decision over discovery. Cut work, don't add it. Default to **scope reduction language**.

### A2. The Perfectionist Creator
- **Job:** Designer, design-engineer, content strategist. Often individual contributor at a recognizable brand.
- **Hours:** 40–50/week, but bleeds into nights "polishing" the side project.
- **Side project window:** Evenings + Sunday afternoons.
- **Money:** $120K–$180K. Side project is creative outlet + future business.
- **Stuck pattern they default to:** Perfectionist.
- **Key tension:** Has redesigned the landing page four times. Hasn't told a single human it exists.
- **Right move from the AI:** Force "ship the dumb version this week." Default to **micro-commitments** + **embarrassment-test** language.

### A3. The Golden Handcuffs Builder
- **Job:** Staff engineer, principal IC, director-track. Often FAANG or late-stage startup.
- **Hours:** 35–45/week (efficient), but mental load is crushing.
- **Side project window:** Weekends. Evenings rare.
- **Money:** $300K–$600K total comp. Equity-loaded. Side project is the **escape hatch**.
- **Stuck pattern they default to:** Scattered Starter (juggles 4 ideas, can't pick one).
- **Key tension:** Too well-paid to take it seriously. Too proud to ship a small version.
- **Right move from the AI:** Force the **"would you ship this if your full-time job paid $40K?"** question. Default to **T03 The Project Selector** before scope. Default to **anti-shame** language about smallness.

### A4. The Accidental Consultant
- **Job:** Solo consultant, fractional exec, ex-PM-now-freelance. 6-figure but volatile.
- **Hours:** 30–50/week, lumpy. Feast/famine cycle.
- **Side project window:** Famine weeks (counterintuitively).
- **Money:** $150K–$300K but unpredictable. Side project is **escape from the hourly trap**.
- **Stuck pattern they default to:** Burnout Cycler.
- **Key tension:** Built a service business by accident. Can't escape billing hours. Wants productized offer or info product.
- **Right move from the AI:** Force the **"productize one engagement"** move. Default to **scoped-out-of-existing-work** framing. **T06 The Pricing Calculator** is high-leverage here.

## Frame B — Stuck Patterns (failure mode, from the quiz)

### B1. The Overcommitter
- **Symptom:** Says yes too much. Calendar always booked. Project time gets eaten by other people's needs.
- **Mental model they need:** "Friction Fences" — make it harder for other people to take your time than to leave it alone.
- **AI move:** Push back hard on any "I'll do it after [X]" deferral. Force a **non-negotiable build block**. Read **T02 The Time Protection Plan** with them.
- **What they don't need:** Another framework. They have plenty.

### B2. The Perfectionist
- **Symptom:** Polishes endlessly. Won't show anyone. Has 12 versions of the homepage in Figma.
- **Mental model they need:** "Micro-Commitments" — promise one human one tiny thing this week.
- **AI move:** Force ship-this-week language. Use the **embarrassment test**: "What would you be willing to show your most discerning friend on Friday?" Read **T07 The Scope Guillotine** with them.
- **What they don't need:** Another design pass.

### B3. The Burnout Cycler
- **Symptom:** Sprint → crash → guilt → sprint. Two-week heroic pushes, three weeks of nothing.
- **Mental model they need:** "Recovery Rhythms" — sustainable pace > heroic pushes.
- **AI move:** Cut session length. Force 90-minute max. Default to **boring-version-ships-forever** language. Read **T02 The Time Protection Plan** with them, especially the shutdown ritual.
- **What they don't need:** Another aggressive plan.

### B4. The Scattered Starter
- **Symptom:** Has four ideas. Can't pick. Starts something every weekend, abandons by Wednesday.
- **Mental model they need:** "Finishing Frameworks" — structure that converts starts into finishes.
- **AI move:** Force **T03 The Project Selector** before anything else. Pick one, kill three. Default to **scope-tight-not-broad** language. Run **T04 The Validation Scorecard** (RICE) only after T03.
- **What they don't need:** Another idea.

## How to detect which persona is in the conversation

The client doesn't always tell you. Listen for these signals:

| Signal | Likely persona |
|---|---|
| "I just had a 7am standup before this" | Overtime Optimizer |
| "I want to make sure the design feels right before I share it" | Perfectionist Creator |
| "My RSU vest is in March so I have time to figure this out" | Golden Handcuffs Builder |
| "My next client doesn't start until July so I'm trying to use this lull" | Accidental Consultant |
| "I'll do it after [external dependency]" | Overcommitter |
| "I need to think more about [aspect]" / "Let me iterate one more time" | Perfectionist |
| "I went hard last weekend and now I'm wiped" | Burnout Cycler |
| "I have like four ideas, I'm not sure which" | Scattered Starter |

If you can't tell, **ask one targeted question**. Not "tell me about yourself" — too broad. Ask: "What got you to start *this* project specifically, vs. the other things you could be doing?" The answer reveals both frames.

---

## What every persona shares

Across all four Tech Personas and all four Stuck Patterns, the brand thesis holds:

- They're **not lazy**. They ship things at work constantly.
- They're **not stupid**. Most are senior PMs, engineers, or operators with 10+ years of experience.
- They're **not under-motivated**. They've already proven motivation isn't the issue.
- They **lack structure for the side project**. Their work has structure (sprints, OKRs, standups, retros, PR review). Their side project doesn't.

The job of the AI Build Partner is to **port the structure they already know works at work** into the home/side context. Not invent new motivation. Not preach.
