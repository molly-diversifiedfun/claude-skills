<stuck_patterns>
Four patterns that keep high-achievers stuck. Each has a named escape route:

**The Overcommitter** — "You're not stuck — you're buried."
- Core cage: Being needed = identity. Calendar full of others' emergencies.
- Escape route: **Friction Fences** — systems that say no for you before you have to.
- Reframe: "What if 'selfish' was actually just... having standards?"
- Examples: inbox full of "quick favors," guilt about saying no, own projects always "next week"

**The Perfectionist** — "You're not stuck — you're stalling."
- Core cage: "Not ready yet" = fear wearing a productivity mask.
- Escape route: **Micro-Commitments** — tiny irreversible actions too small to fail.
- Reframe: "What if you're not waiting until you're ready — you're waiting until you can't fail?"
- Examples: rewritten same draft dozens of times, weeks of research before action, V1 never sees light of day

**The Burnout Cycler** — "You're not stuck — you're depleted."
- Core cage: Two speeds: 1000% or collapsed. Rest = failure.
- Escape route: **Recovery Rhythms** — rest built INTO the work, not after it.
- Reframe: "What if rest wasn't the reward for productivity — but the reason for it?"
- Examples: two speeds (on fire or burned out), rest feels like failing, boom-bust cycles

**The Scattered Starter** — "You're not stuck — you're split."
- Core cage: Starting > finishing. 47 things at 30%. Addicted to possibility.
- Escape route: **Finishing Frameworks** — make completion more compelling than novelty.
- Reframe: "What if your next big thing is actually finishing one of your current things?"
- Examples: notes app graveyard, excited about new before finishing old, follow-through feels like a flaw
</stuck_patterns>

<infrastructure_audit>
Eight questions to score project infrastructure. Ask conversationally, not as a checklist.

1. Do you have a specific, non-negotiable ship date?
2. Can you describe your V1 outcome in a single sentence?
3. Have you broken the project into 3-5 major milestones?
4. Do you have a weekly check-in scheduled with someone?
5. Have you told at least one person your ship date?
6. Is your next single action identified and actually scheduled?
7. Do you have a written "NOT NOW" list for features you're deferring?
8. Do you have dedicated, recurring time blocks for project work?

**Scoring (track internally):**
- YES = 1 point per question
- 6-8: **Fortress** — strong infrastructure, problem is elsewhere
- 3-5: **Leaky Fence** — some structure, gaps letting you slip
- 0-2: **Open Door** — no infrastructure, this IS the core problem
</infrastructure_audit>

<followability_ladder>
Identify which rung is broken:

- **Understand** — no clarity on what they're building or why
- **Believe** — no trust in their own plan, keep changing it
- **Do** — can't start, first step feels too big
- **Repeat** — can't sustain, starts strong then fades after a week
</followability_ladder>

<jtbd>
Jobs-to-Be-Done statement format:
"When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [outcome]."

Does not mention the product. It's about the customer's situation. Kills 80% of scope creep — features either solve the core job or they're V2.
</jtbd>

<scope_creep_detector>
Five questions. If ANY fails, the feature is V2:

1. Is this absolutely essential for V1 Core Outcome to function?
2. Can V1 ship without this?
3. Does this add more than a day of work?
4. Am I adding this because I'm avoiding a harder task?
5. Will I be proud to have shipped V1 without this?
</scope_creep_detector>

<decomposition_method>
From blob to step:

- **Level 1:** 3-5 Milestones (verb-first: "User can log in" not "Authentication")
- **Level 2:** Break milestones into tasks (60-120 minutes each)
- **Level 3:** Your very next action. Schedule it.

Your brain cannot execute a blob. It can only execute a step.
</decomposition_method>

<rice_scoring>
Score each dimension 1-10:

**RICE = (Reach x Impact x Confidence) / Effort**

- **Below 50:** Kill it. Not worth your time.
- **50-150:** Proceed with caution. Validate harder.
- **Above 150:** Strong signal. Build it.
</rice_scoring>

<ten_conversation_method>
Real validation from real humans. Exactly 10 conversations, right people, real-time.

**5 Core Questions:**
1. "Walk me through your current workflow for [problem]. What's the messiest part?"
2. "How much time do you spend dealing with this?"
3. "Have you tried existing solutions? What happened?"
4. "What would an ideal solution look like?"
5. "If I built [very basic version], would you use it?"

**Signal thresholds:**
- 8+/10 say yes = strong signal, build it
- 4-7/10 = mixed, refine the offer
- 3 or fewer = no signal, kill it or pivot
</ten_conversation_method>

<daily_build_protocol>
**Startup Ritual (5 minutes):**
1. Phone in another room. Do Not Disturb on.
2. Review your V1 core outcome.
3. Check the exact task you wrote down yesterday.
4. Open the file. Don't check email.

**Park Downhill:**
At the end of your session, write down the EXACT next action for tomorrow. Be specific.
- Bad: "Work on Module 2."
- Good: "Write the opening paragraph of Module 2: the 'Why This Matters' section."

**Shutdown Ritual (5 minutes):**
1. Stop. Resist "just one more thing."
2. Commit and save your work.
3. Park downhill — write tomorrow's next action.
4. Close all project tabs.
5. Take 10 seconds to acknowledge you showed up.
</daily_build_protocol>

<seventy_thirty_ai_rule>
AI writes 70% (first draft, structure, ideas). You make it yours in the last 30% (editing for voice, adding personal examples, fact-checking).

AI doesn't solve your problem because it writes for you. It solves your problem because it destroys the blank page. Fixing is action. Action is momentum. Momentum ships products.

**Non-negotiable:** The product must sound like you.
</seventy_thirty_ai_rule>

<six_week_structure>
- **Week 1: Set up** — Fix audit gaps, block time, tell someone your ship date
- **Week 2: Validate** — Talk to 3-5 real people, finalize scope
- **Weeks 3-4: Build** — One feature at a time, most important first
- **Week 5: Launch prep** — Landing page, listing, email — whatever fits
- **Week 6: Ship it** — Final polish, first real user/customer, celebrate
</six_week_structure>

<anti_patterns_summary>
Key concepts from the 10 anti-pattern essays — use when diagnosing or educating:

1. **Expert Tax** — "I can do it myself" costs six figures. You're a Porsche hooked to a plow.
2. **Discipline Delusion** — Willpower depletes hourly. Systems stay at 100%. Hope is not a business model.
3. **System Fatigue** — 14 productivity apps = optimizing procrastination. Napkin test: if you can't explain it in 2 min, it's too complex.
4. **Infrastructure Myth** — You don't have a motivation problem, you have a plumbing problem. Three-legged stool: Vision + Willpower + System (you're missing the system).
5. **Perfectionism Pivot** — High standards create value. Perfectionism proves YOU'RE valuable. 70% Rule: go.
6. **Busy Trap** — 12-hour days, can't name what moved needle by $10K. $10K Question before every task.
7. **Decision Fatigue** — Choice = friction with better marketing. Eliminate 80% of decisions, don't make them better.
8. **Lone Wolf** — Solopreneur = bottleneck. Architect vs Expert: income capped by calendar vs imagination.
9. **Good Enough Trap** — "Personal touch" = unscalable. Gordon Ramsay doesn't cook your dinner. Systematize your standards.
10. **Wait for Clarity** — Clarity is built through action, not found through planning. OODA Loop. Type 1 vs Type 2 decisions.
</anti_patterns_summary>
